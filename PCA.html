Let’s plot the proportion of the variation explained for each of the PCs. We call this plot an elbow or scree plot. Ideally, we want to see an elbow someplace where we start to get diminishing returns. From this scree plot, we can see a subtle elbow at \(k = 2\) and \(k = 5\) or 6. Let’s choose \(k = 2\) for simplicity (this is often the case when it’s not obvious).
